About Me

I’m an AI student with a strong curiosity for how intelligent systems actually work beneath the surface. What started as an interest in machine learning gradually turned into a deeper focus on Deep Learning, especially in areas where models can have real-world impact.

I don’t enjoy just “using” models — I like understanding them, modifying them, and pushing them further. Whether it’s experimenting with architectures or improving performance, I’m always trying to go beyond the basic implementation.

My work spans multiple layers of software — from backend systems to low-level hardware — but I naturally gravitate toward deep learning applications, particularly in NLP and computer vision.

Recently, I’ve been working on more advanced ideas like multi-modal systems (e.g. combining CNNs for images with MLPs for tabular data), and thinking more about how different types of data can be integrated into a single model.

I also have experience building full-stack systems, which helps me think beyond just the model — I care about how things are deployed, used, and scaled in real environments.

Beyond technical work, I’ve pushed myself into competitive and high-pressure environments — from programming contests to presenting projects on stage.
